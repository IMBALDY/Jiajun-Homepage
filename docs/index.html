About Me

I am Jiajun Chen, currently a junior undergraduate student majoring in Data Science and Big Data Technology at the School of Computer Science, Beijing University of Posts and Telecommunications. My research interests include Multimodal Learning, Intention Grounding, Multimodal Distillation, Large Language Models (LLMs), and Reinforcement Learning.

My journey in AI began in 2017 when I watched AlphaGo defeat Ke Jie, the world's top Go player. That moment profoundly impacted me and ignited my passion for artificial intelligence. I am dedicated to contributing to this field and firmly believe that artificial intelligence will transcend textual limitations to perceive the world in human-like ways across multiple modalities.

I am currently working on research projects focusing on multimodal representation learning with missing modalities and multimodal distillation. I am actively seeking opportunities for graduate studies in the field of AI, particularly at the National University of Singapore.

üè´Research Experience

  • DREAM: Dynamic Routing for Embedding Alignment in Multimodal Distillation
    Feb 2025 - Present, Beihang University, Beijing, China
    • Designed and developed DREAM, a novel framework addressing three key challenges in multimodal multi-task distillation: conflicting objectives across tasks, unclear cross-modal associations, and modal feature distribution alignment issues
    • Created an innovative three-stage distillation strategy comprising feature distillation, relation distillation with dynamic routing, and response distillation with adaptive weighting
    • Implemented a lightweight task-aware routing network that dynamically selects optimal distillation pathways based on input modalities and task requirements
    • Pioneered a multimodal knowledge decoupling approach that first transfers shared semantic knowledge before fine-tuning modality-specific modules
    • Conducted comprehensive experiments across multiple vision-language datasets, demonstrating superior performance over existing distillation methods
  • PROMISE: Prompt-Attentive Hierarchical Contrastive Learning for Robust Cross-Modal Representation with Missing Modalities
    Mar 2024 - Apr 2025, Beijing University of Posts and Telecommunications, Beijing, China
    • Led the development of PROMISE, a novel multimodal framework addressing the critical challenge of missing modalities in cross-modal learning
    • Independently designed and implemented an innovative prompt attention mechanism that dynamically synthesizes robust representations for missing modalities
    • Pioneered a hierarchical contrastive learning approach with original dual-level objective design (inter-modal and intra-modal)
    • Achieved significant performance improvements of 2-3% in accuracy and F1 scores across diverse benchmark datasets (UPMC-Food-101, HatefulMemes, MM-IMDB, N24News)
    • Reviewed over 40 research papers and implemented various architectures like Transformer, CLIP, and LLaVA to enhance understanding of multimodal systems
  • Solving Real World Problems with Computational Thinking
